# Pain → Agitate → Solve (PAS)

## Core Logic

Define a problem the audience already feels → amplify the emotional weight until inaction becomes unbearable → introduce the product as the inevitable remedy. The product earns its entrance by arriving only after the viewer is primed to want it.

## Emotional Arc Pattern

```
Anxiety/Frustration ──▶ Peak Tension ──▶ Relief ──▶ Confidence ──▶ Empowerment
     (hook)            (agitation)     (reveal)    (demo)          (CTA)
```

Canonical PAS samples verbalize this as: "Overwhelm and frustration transition into relief, control, and triumphant success" (Zeliq), "Frustration with manual processes shifting to relief and excitement" (JustCall), "Intense frustration and anxiety → relief and clarity → empowerment and control" (HRS). The common pattern is a **V-curve**: dip into a named negative beat → product arrives as the pivot → sustained ascent through control / confidence / empowerment.

## Typical Section Sequence

| Order | Type              | Role                                                                   | Approx % |
| ----- | ----------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------- |
| 1     | hook              | Establish the problem with data or scenario                            | 5-15%    |
| 2     | pain_point        | Amplify: show emotional/financial cost                                 | 10-20%   |
| 3     | benefit_highlight | Tease the solution concept (not the product yet)                       | 5-10%    |
| 4     | product_intro     | Reveal the product as the vehicle for the solution                     | 10-15%   |
| 5     | feature_showcase  | Demo the product solving the pain (UI sequence — 3+ scenes; see below) | 25-50%   |
| 6     | cta               | Inspire action                                                         | 10-15%   |

**Reveal timing** — PAS is the late-reveal archetype. Across the reverse-engineered samples, product reveal lands at:

- Madison: 33% (scene 5 of 15) — extended pain to deepen relief contrast
- ResponseScribe: 43% (scene 3 of 7)
- Kyvos: 38% (scene 5 of 13) — sells category before product (overlaps with BAB)
- JustCall IQ: 50% (scene 4 of 8) — full half of the runtime on agitation
- PayCloud: 33% (scene 3 of 9)
- affable.ai (original): 71% (scene 8 of 11) — extreme late reveal, demo-heavy explainer

**Strategic logic**: longer agitation builds psychological investment in the problem before the hero product arrives, increasing relief contrast. Don't reveal early in PAS — you'd be solving a problem the audience hasn't felt yet.

## When to Use

- The audience already experiences the pain (B2B broken workflows, operational friction)
- You have strong data points (industry stats, cost figures) to quantify the problem
- The product is a direct remedy to a specific frustration
- You need to build urgency before the reveal

## When NOT to Use

- The product creates a new category nobody knows they need yet (→ use Future Pacing or BAB)
- The product's UI demo is the strongest selling point (→ use Demo Loop)
- The audience has no existing pain awareness
- The product is desire-driven (NFTs, collectibles, luxury) rather than relief-driven (→ use Feature-Benefit Cascade)

## Common compound

PAS very often runs **Feature-Benefit Cascade as its inner rhythm** during the showcase phase — alternating `feature_showcase` ↔ `benefit_highlight` for 6+ consecutive scenes. Write the archetype string as `"Problem-Agitate-Solution (PAS) with Feature-Benefit progression"` when this is the case. The Madison reverse-engineered file labels itself exactly this way.

## Hook strategies that work for PAS

From the reverse-engineered samples:

- **Shocking statistic** — PayCloud "50% of companies still rely on paper checks"; Fosfor opens with industry-trend social validation ("Everyone is talking about the promise of AI")
- **Pain validation** — ResponseScribe "Responding to all of your online reviews", JustCall "Tired of clueless conversations?", Alpha "The world of crypto is moving fast"
- **Direct address / character hail** — JustCall "Hey, sales pro.", JustCall IQ "Sales teams, listen up!"
- **Visceral metaphor** — HRS "Goodbye to long airport queues, goodbye to dinosaurs of the past" (anaphora + escalation from concrete to abstract)
- **Relatable scenario** — Madison "In today's world, the internet is where consumers discover local businesses..."

Don't open PAS with "Imagine..." or visual-spectacle hooks — those belong to Future Pacing and Feature-Benefit Cascade respectively.

## Persuasion techniques that recur

The PAS samples cluster around: **Pain agitation**, **Contrast (chaos → clean)**, **Empathy / internal monologue**, **Relief via contrast**, **Friction reduction**, **Risk reversal (free trial)**, **Statistical proof / authority by association with logos**, **Demonstrated efficiency**, **Empowerment & control**.

Two pinned moves:

- **Overwhelm Cloud + Invoice Shock** (Madison): visually stack floating task bubbles around the protagonist → cut to ticking financial counter → snap to product. Pain in two registers (mental + financial) before reveal.
- **Tribal-Vocabulary Saturation + Cultural-Ritual Climax** (Alpha): every script line uses audience-native terms ("signal vs noise", "hot", "gm", `.wtf` TLD); then _elevate_ a community-bonding ritual (the GM button) to climax position over utilitarian capabilities. Insider fluency proves the brand belongs in the tribe.
